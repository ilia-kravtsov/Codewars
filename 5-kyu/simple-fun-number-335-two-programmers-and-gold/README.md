<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/59549d482a68fe3bc2000146/train/javascript target="_blank">Simple Fun #335: Two Programmers And Gold</a></h2><h3>5 kyu</h3><h2 id="task">Task</h2><p>In the field, two programmers A and B found some gold at the same time. They all wanted the gold, and they decided to use the following rules to distribute gold:</p><p>They divided gold into n piles and be in line. The amount of each pile and the order of piles all are randomly.</p><p>They took turns to take away a pile of gold from the far left or the far right.</p><p>In each round, the programmer will use his wisdom to choose the gold that is best for him.</p><h3 id="input">Input</h3><p>A list<sup>*</sup> of integers representing gold.</p><h3 id="output">Output</h3><p>Calculated final amount of gold obtained by A and B in a tuple<sup>*</sup> ( amount of A, amount of B ).</p><p><sup>*</sup>list / tuple representation varies with language - see Example tests</p><p>Note, we can assume that A always takes first, and each time they used the best strategy.</p><h3 id="example">Example</h3><p>For <code>gold = [10,1000,2,1]</code>, the output should be <code>[1001,12]</code>.</p><pre><code>At 1st turn, A can take 10 or 1, 10 is greater than 1.Should we choose 10? No, clever programmer don't think so ;-)Because if A choose 10, next turn B can choose 1000.So, A choose 1 is the best choice.At 2nd turn, Whether B chooses 10 or 2, in the 3rd turn, A can always choose 1000.So, B choose 10 is the best choice.Final result:A: 1 + 1000 = 1001B: 10 + 2 = 12</code></pre><p>For <code>golds = [10,1000,2]</code>, the output should be <code>[12,1000]</code>.</p><p>In this example, the choice A faces is the same as the 2nd turn in the previous example.</p>
