<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/567cd7da4b9255b96b000022/train/javascript target="_blank">Squarefree Part of a Number</a></h2><h3>5 kyu</h3><p>The squarefree part of a positive integer is the largest divisor of that integer which itself has no square factors (other than 1). For example, the squareefree part of 12 is 6, since the only larger divisor is 12, and 12 has a square factor (namely, 4).</p><p>Your challenge, should you choose to accept it, is to implement a <code>squareFreePart</code> function which accepts a number <code>n</code> and returns the squarefree part of <code>n</code>. </p><p>In the case that <code>n = 1</code>, your function should return 1. The input will always be a strictly positive integer.</p><p>Here are some examples (input ---&gt; output:</p><pre><code> 2  ---&gt; 2 4  ---&gt; 2 24 ---&gt;  6 (since any larger divisor is divisible by 4, which is a square)</code></pre><p>Good luck!</p>
