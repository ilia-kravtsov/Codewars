<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/562b099becfe844f3800000a/train/javascript target="_blank">Ratio of Bouncy Numbers</a></h2><h3>5 kyu</h3><p>Working from left-to-right if no digit is exceeded by the digit to its left it is called an increasing number; for example, <code>134468</code>.</p><p>Similarly if no digit is exceeded by the digit to its right it is called a decreasing number; for example, <code>66420</code>.</p><p>We shall call a positive integer that is neither increasing nor decreasing a "bouncy" number; for example, <code>155349</code>.</p><p>Clearly there cannot be any bouncy numbers below one-hundred, but just over half of the numbers below one-thousand (<code>525</code>) are bouncy. In fact, the least number for which the proportion of bouncy numbers first reaches 50% is <code>538</code>.</p><p>Surprisingly, bouncy numbers become more and more common and by the time we reach <code>21780</code> the proportion of bouncy numbers is equal to 90%.</p><h4 id="your-task">Your Task</h4><p>Complete the <code>bouncyRatio</code> function.</p><p>The input will be the target ratio.</p><p>The output should be the smallest number such that the proportion of bouncy numbers reaches the target ratio.</p><p>You should throw an <code>Error</code> for a ratio less than 0% or greater than 99%.</p><p><strong>Source</strong></p><ul><li><a href="https://projecteuler.net/problem=112" data-turbolinks="false" target="_blank">https://projecteuler.net/problem=112</a></li></ul><p><strong>Updates</strong></p><ul><li>26/10/2015: Added a higher precision test case.</li></ul>
